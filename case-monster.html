Onboarding

Once accepted, athletes receive a contract summary modal with their rank, sport, and offer details — followed by a three-step welcome sequence that explains how to build a resume, get paid, and access the Monster gear shop. The flow was designed to eliminate the onboarding support load that previously overwhelmed team managers.

Accept Your Offer Modal

Contract summary including sport, rank, offer percentage, and monthly allotments (Monster cases + gear items). One-tap accept.

Welcome to Monster Army

3-card carousel: Build your resume → Start Getting Paid → Shop Cool Gear. Each card surfaced the primary action for that feature.

Lessons Learned

speed
Speed over Symmetry

In action sports, the "perfect" UI is secondary to real-time data ingestion. We learned that reduced latency in content delivery had a higher brand impact than complex visual layouts.

diversity_3
Cultural-Technical Alignment

Building tools for athletes required a mobile-first, "glove-friendly" design approach. High-contrast indicators were essential for readability in high-glare training environments.

integration_instructions
API Agnosticism

The biggest friction wasn't code; it was legacy legal structures. Designing the "Quick-Claw" framework required close collaboration with legal teams to bake compliance directly into the UI.

query_stats
Direct Correlation Metrics

We moved from vanity social metrics to regional sales spikes. Integrating localized e-commerce data allowed the team to see the immediate dollar value of every athlete stunt.
